Emulsification

Emulsification is the technique of combining two immiscible liquids — typically a fat and a water-based liquid — into a stable, unified mixture. It's one of the most important techniques in cooking, underpinning everything from mayonnaise and hollandaise to vinaigrettes and pasta sauces.

The science: Oil and water don't naturally mix because oil molecules are non-polar and water molecules are polar. An emulsifier (like lecithin in egg yolks, or mustard proteins) has both a hydrophilic (water-loving) end and a hydrophobic (fat-loving) end. The emulsifier positions itself at the oil-water interface, surrounding tiny oil droplets and preventing them from coalescing.

Types of emulsions:
- Oil-in-water (O/W): oil droplets dispersed in water. Examples: hollandaise, beurre blanc, mayonnaise, vinaigrette, pasta carbonara sauce
- Water-in-oil (W/O): water droplets in oil. Example: butter

Key emulsifiers in cooking:
- Egg yolk lecithin — the most common. Used in mayo, hollandaise, carbonara
- Mustard — dijon mustard contains mucilage that helps stabilize vinaigrettes
- Honey — works as an emulsifier in dressings
- Starchy pasta water — the starch acts as an emulsifier in cacio e pepe and carbonara

How to create a stable emulsion:
1. Start with one liquid in your bowl/pan
2. Add the second liquid VERY slowly at first — drop by drop if needed
3. Whisk or blend constantly and vigorously
4. Temperature matters: most emulsions work best at room temperature or slightly warm. Too hot and egg yolks scramble; too cold and the fat solidifies

Broken emulsions:
If your emulsion breaks (separates), you can often rescue it by:
- Starting fresh with a new egg yolk in a clean bowl
- Slowly whisking the broken sauce into the new yolk
- Adding a small amount of boiling water and whisking vigorously

Common applications: 
- Mayonnaise: oil emulsified into egg yolk
- Hollandaise: melted butter emulsified into egg yolks with lemon juice
- Beurre blanc: cold butter whisked into white wine reduction
- Carbonara: egg yolks emulsified with pasta water and pork fat
- Vinaigrette: oil and vinegar (unstable, needs shaking before use unless mustard is added)

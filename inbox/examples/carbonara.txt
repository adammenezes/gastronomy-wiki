Pasta Carbonara

This is one of Rome's greatest pasta dishes. Real carbonara uses guanciale (cured pork cheek), not bacon. The sauce is made purely from eggs, Pecorino Romano, and black pepper — no cream whatsoever.

Ingredients (serves 2):
- 200g spaghetti or rigatoni
- 150g guanciale, cut into lardons
- 3 large egg yolks + 1 whole egg
- 60g Pecorino Romano, finely grated (plus extra to serve)
- Freshly cracked black pepper (generous)
- Salt for pasta water

Method:
1. Bring a large pot of well-salted water to a boil. Cook the pasta until just al dente. Reserve at least 1 cup of pasta water before draining.
2. While pasta cooks, render the guanciale in a cold pan over medium heat. The fat should melt slowly. Cook until crispy and golden. Remove from heat and let cool slightly.
3. In a bowl, whisk together the egg yolks, whole egg, and Pecorino Romano until combined. Season generously with black pepper.
4. Add the drained pasta directly to the pan with the guanciale (off heat). Toss to coat in the pork fat.
5. Pour the egg mixture over the pasta, tossing constantly and adding pasta water a splash at a time. The residual heat should create a silky, creamy sauce — not scrambled eggs. Work fast.
6. Serve immediately with extra Pecorino and black pepper.

Tips:
- The key is temperature control. Too hot and you get scrambled eggs. Take the pan fully off heat before adding the egg mixture.
- Guanciale is non-negotiable for authenticity but pancetta works in a pinch. Never use bacon.
- Some Romans use only Pecorino, others use a mix of Pecorino and Parmigiano. I prefer all Pecorino for the sharpness.
- The technique of emulsifying the egg with the starchy pasta water is what makes the sauce silky.

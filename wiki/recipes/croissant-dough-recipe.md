## Overview
Croissant Dough is a classic yeast-leavened [[Laminated Dough]] from [[French Cuisine]], renowned for its exceptionally light, flaky, and buttery texture. The signature honeycomb crumb is achieved through the meticulous process of [[Lamination]], where a block of butter, known as the [[Beurrage]], is folded and rolled into the dough multiple times to create hundreds of distinct, alternating layers. This recipe is attributed to chef [[Kurt Fogle]].

## Ingredients
*Note: The original source did not specify quantities.*
- [[Flour]]
- [[Sugar]]
- [[Yeast]]
- [[Butter]] (for the dough, or *détrempe*)
- [[Salt]]
- [[Water]]
- [[Milk]]
- [[Butter]] (for the [[Beurrage]], or butter block)
- [[Egg Wash]]

## Method
1.  **Mix the Dough:** In the bowl of a [[Mixer]] fitted with a [[Dough Hook]], combine the [[Flour]], [[Sugar]], [[Yeast]], [[Butter]], and [[Salt]]. Turn the mixer on low and gradually add the [[Water]] and [[Milk]]. Mix for 5-6 minutes until the dough is smooth and soft. Turn the dough out onto a [[Parchment Paper]]-lined [[Sheet Pan]] and shape it into a rectangle. Allow the dough to rest for 1-2 hours to undergo [[Primary Fermentation]].

2.  **Chill the Dough & Prep the Beurrage:** After the dough has doubled in bulk, gently deflate it and cover with [[Plastic Wrap]]. Chill for at least 1 hour, or overnight (12-24 hours). While the dough is chilling, prepare the [[Beurrage]]. Place the butter block between two sheets of [[Parchment Paper]], pound it with a [[Rolling Pin]] to soften, and roll it into a rectangle approximately two-thirds the size of the chilled dough rectangle.

3.  **Lamination:** On a lightly floured surface, roll the chilled dough into a large rectangle. Encase the [[Beurrage]] within the dough using your preferred method (e.g., single fold, envelope fold). This is the "lock-in" step. Proceed with the [[Lamination]] process by performing a total of three turns (e.g., [[Letter Fold]] or [[Book Fold]]), chilling the dough for 30-45 minutes in the refrigerator between each turn to ensure the butter remains solid.

4.  **Cut and Shape:** After the final turn and rest, roll the dough out to the desired thickness. Cut and shape as desired for [[Croissant]], [[Pain au Chocolat]], or other pastries. Brush the shaped pastries with [[Egg Wash]]. Allow them to undergo a final [[Proofing]] until they have doubled in bulk and appear airy. Bake at 375-400°F (190-205°C) until golden brown and cooked through.

## Chef's Notes & Variations
- **Temperature Control:** The success of [[Laminated Dough]] hinges on temperature. The dough and the [[Beurrage]] must remain cold throughout the process. If the [[Butter]] becomes too warm, it will melt into the dough, and the distinct layers will be lost.
- **Beurrage Consistency:** The butter block should be pliable but cold when you lock it in—ideally the same consistency as the chilled dough. This prevents it from cracking or squeezing out during rolling.
- **Resting is Crucial:** Do not skip the chilling periods between folds. This allows the [[Gluten]] in the dough to relax, making it easier to roll out without tearing, and keeps the butter solid.
- **Applications:** This dough is the foundation for many classic pastries, including [[Croissant]], [[Pain au Chocolat]], [[Danish Pastry]], and [[Kouign-Amann]].

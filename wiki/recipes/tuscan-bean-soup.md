## Overview
[[Tuscan Bean Soup]], or *Zuppa Toscana*, is a hearty and rustic [[Italian Cuisine]] [[dish]] originating from the [[Tuscany]] region. It typically features [[Cannellini Beans]], leafy [[greens]] like [[Kale]] or [[Swiss Chard]], and often [[Pancetta]] or [[Guanciale]] for [[depth of flavor]]. This comforting [[Soup]] embodies the *cucina povera* (peasant [[cooking]]) philosophy, transforming simple, readily available [[ingredients]] into a nourishing and flavorful [[meal]], often served with [[crusty bread]]. ## Ingredients
*   2 tbsp [[Olive Oil]]
*   4 oz [[Pancetta]], diced
*   1 large [[Yellow Onion]], chopped
*   2 cloves [[Garlic]], minced
*   1 large [[Carrot]], diced
*   2 stalks [[Celery]], diced
*   1 tbsp [[Tomato Paste]]
*   6 cups [[Vegetable Broth]] or [[Chicken Broth]]
*   2 (15-oz) cans [[Cannellini Beans]], rinsed and drained
*   1 bunch [[Kale]] (about 6 oz), tough stems removed, chopped
*   1 tsp dried [[Rosemary]]
*   [[Salt]] and freshly ground [[Black Pepper]] to taste
*   [[Parmesan Cheese]] or [[Pecorino Romano]], for serving (optional)

## Method
1.  Heat the [[Olive Oil]] in a large [[Dutch Oven]] or heavy-bottomed [[Soup Pot]] over medium [[heat]]. Add the diced [[Pancetta]] and [[Render]] until [[crispy]], about 5-7 minutes. Remove the crispy [[pancetta]] with a [[Slotted Spoon]] and set aside, leaving the [[rendered fat]] in the [[pot]].
2.  Add the chopped [[Yellow Onion]], diced [[Carrot]], and diced [[Celery]] to the [[pot]]. [[Sauté]] for 8-10 minutes, or until the [[vegetables]] have softened and the [[onion]] is [[translucent]].
3.  Stir in the minced [[Garlic]] and [[Tomato Paste]]. Cook for another 2 minutes, stirring constantly, until the [[tomato paste]] darkens slightly and becomes fragrant. This step helps to deepen the [[Umami]] [[flavor]].
4.  Pour in the [[Vegetable Broth]] and add the rinsed [[Cannellini Beans]] and dried [[Rosemary]]. Bring the mixture to a [[boil]], then reduce the [[heat]] to low, cover, and [[Simmer]] for 20 minutes to allow the [[flavors]] to meld.
5.  Add the chopped [[Kale]] to the [[pot]]. Continue to [[Simmer]] for another 10-15 minutes, or until the [[kale]] is [[tender]] but still [[vibrant]].
6.  Taste the [[Soup]] and adjust [[seasoning]] with [[Salt]] and [[Black Pepper]] as needed. [[Ladle]] into [[bowls]] and garnish with the reserved [[crispy]] [[Pancetta]] and a sprinkle of grated [[Parmesan Cheese]] or [[Pecorino Romano]], if desired. Serve hot with [[crusty bread]].

## Chef's Notes & Variations
*   **Bean Choice:** While [[Cannellini Beans]] are traditional, other [[white beans]] like [[Great Northern Beans]] or [[Navy Beans]] can be substituted. For a [[creamier]] [[texture]], you can [[Purée]] about a quarter of the [[beans]] before adding them to the [[soup]], or mash some against the side of the [[pot]] during [[simmering]].
*   **Greens:** If [[Kale]] is unavailable, [[Swiss Chard]] or even [[Spinach]] can be used. Add [[spinach]] closer to the end of [[cooking]] as it [[wilts]] much faster.
*   **Meat Options:** For a [[vegetarian]] version, simply omit the [[Pancetta]] and use a good quality [[Olive Oil]] to start. You can also add [[Italian Sausage]] (browned and crumbled) for a different [[flavor profile]].
*   **Flavor Boost:** A [[Parmesan Rind]] added during the [[simmering]] step can infuse the [[soup]] with extra [[Umami]] and [[depth]]. Remember to remove it before serving.
*   **Serving Suggestion:** This [[Soup]] is excellent served with a drizzle of high-quality [[Extra Virgin Olive Oil]] and a sprinkle of [[Red Pepper Flakes]] for a touch of [[heat]].

## Overview
[[Gratin Dauphinoise]], also known as [[Pommes Dauphinoise]], is a celebrated [[French Cuisine]] dish originating from the [[Dauphiné]] region in southeastern [[France]]. It features thinly [[Slice|sliced]] [[Potatoes]] [[Layering|layered]] in a [[Baking Dish]] and [[Bake|baked]] until tender with a rich mixture of [[Heavy Cream]], [[Milk]], and [[Garlic]]. This dish is a classic example of a [[Gratin]], known for its creamy interior and often a golden-brown top.

## Ingredients
*   **Potatoes**: 4-5 medium (e.g., [[Russets]] or [[Yukon Gold]])
*   **Heavy Cream**: 2 cups
*   **Milk**: 1 cup
*   **Garlic**: 2-3 cloves, minced
*   **Salt**: To taste
*   **Pepper**: To taste
*   **Nutmeg**: A pinch (optional)
*   **Oil** or [[Melted Butter]]: For greasing the dish
*   **Gruyère Cheese**: 1/2 cup, grated (optional, for topping)

## Method

### Preparation of Potatoes
1.  [[Peel]] the [[Potatoes]] using a [[Vegetable Peeler]] and [[Slice]] them thinly, about 1/8 inch thick. A [[Mandoline Slicer]] can be used for even slices, otherwise a [[Chef Knife]] and [[Cutting Board]] will suffice.

### Preparation of Cream Mixture
2.  In a [[Saucepan]], [[Combine]] the [[Heavy Cream]], [[Milk]], and [[Minced Garlic]] cloves. [[Season]] the mixture with [[Salt]], [[Pepper]], and a pinch of [[Nutmeg]] for flavor. Heat the mixture to a gentle [[Simmer]], then remove from heat.

### Layering
3.  [[Preheat]] the [[Oven]] to 375°F (190°C). [[Grease]] a [[Baking Dish]] (or a 2” half-size [[Hotel Pan]]) generously with [[Butter]] or [[Oil]]. [[Arrange]] a layer of [[Sliced Potatoes]] in the bottom of the [[Baking Dish]], overlapping slightly. Pour a portion of the cream mixture over the [[Potatoes]], ensuring they are evenly coated. Repeat the [[Layering]] process with the remaining [[Potatoes]] and cream mixture until the [[Baking Dish]] is filled, finishing with a layer of cream.

### Baking
4.  Cover the [[Baking Dish]] with [[Parchment Paper]] and [[Aluminum Foil]]. [[Bake]] in the preheated [[Oven]] for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the [[Potatoes]] are [[Tender]] when pierced with a fork. Remove the [[Foil]] and [[Parchment Paper]] during the last 10-15 minutes of [[Baking]] to allow the top to [[Brown]] slightly.

### Optional Cheese Topping
5.  If desired, [[Sprinkle]] [[Grated Gruyère Cheese]] over the top of the [[Potatoes]] during the last 10-15 minutes of [[Baking]]. This adds a deliciously [[Cheesy Crust]] to the dish.

### Serving
6.  Once [[Bake|baked]], allow the [[Pommes Dauphinoise]] to [[Cool]] for a few minutes before serving. [[Garnish]] with [[Fresh Herbs]], such as [[Parsley]] or [[Chives]], for added flavor and presentation. Serve the [[Pommes Dauphinoise]] as a decadent [[Side Dish]] alongside [[Roasted Meats]] and [[Poultry]] or as part of a [[Vegetarian Meal]].

### Optional Serving Method (for make-ahead)
7.  To prepare ahead, [[Compress]] the [[Gratin]] with a [[Weight]] and [[Cool Completely]]. [[Portion]] the [[Gratin]] onto a [[Sheet Pan]], [[Sprinkle]] with [[Gruyère Cheese]], and [[Reheat]] at 375°F (190°C) for about 20 minutes.

## Chef's Notes & Variations
The choice of [[Potatoes]] is crucial for [[Gratin Dauphinoise]]; starchy varieties like [[Russets]] or waxy ones like [[Yukon Gold]] work best, providing both structure and creaminess. This classic [[French]] preparation can be adapted to various [[Root Vegetables]] and [[Tuber Vegetables]], such as [[Sweet Potatoes]], [[Celery Root]], or [[Parsnips]], either individually or in combination. Traditionally made with [[Heavy Cream]], variations sometimes include the addition of [[Egg Yolks]] to the cream to create a richer [[Custard]]-like texture, or the substitution of a [[Flavorful Stock]] for cream, as seen in [[Gratin Savoyard]]. Ensuring the potatoes are sliced evenly is key for consistent cooking, and a [[Mandoline Slicer]] is highly recommended for this.

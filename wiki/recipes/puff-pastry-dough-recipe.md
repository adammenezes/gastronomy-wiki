## Overview
Puff Pastry, known in [[French Cuisine]] as *pâte feuilletée*, is a classic [[Laminated Dough]] celebrated for its hundreds of light, flaky layers. The signature texture is achieved through a meticulous process of layering a block of dough (the *[[Détrempe]]*) with a block of butter (the *[[Beurrage]]*) and repeatedly folding and rolling the package. This technique creates distinct, separate layers of fat and dough that puff up dramatically when baked as the water in the butter and dough turns to steam.

## Ingredients
**For the Détrempe (Dough Block):**
*   [[Flour]]
*   [[Water]]
*   [[Salt]]

**For the Beurrage (Butter Block):**
*   Softened [[Butter]]
*   2 oz. / 30 g [[Flour]]

## Method
1.  **Prepare the Détrempe:** In a [[Mixer]] fitted with a [[Dough Hook]] attachment, combine the [[Flour]], [[Water]], and [[Salt]]. Mix until a smooth, cohesive dough forms. Shape the dough into a square, dust it lightly with flour, and place it on a [[Parchment Paper]]-lined [[Sheet Pan]]. Cover with [[Plastic Wrap]] and chill in the refrigerator for 1 hour.

2.  **Prepare the Beurrage:** In a separate [[Mixing Bowl]] with a [[Paddle Attachment]], blend the softened [[Butter]] and 2 oz. / 30 g of flour until smooth and homogenous. Transfer this mixture to a sheet of parchment paper and form it into an even square approximately ½ inch (1 cm) thick. Cover with plastic wrap and chill until it is firm but still pliable, not hard.

3.  **Encase the Butter:** On a lightly floured surface, roll the chilled détrempe into a square large enough to enclose the beurrage. Place the beurrage diagonally in the center of the dough. Fold the corners of the dough over the butter to meet in the center, creating an [[Envelope Fold]]. Pinch the seams to seal the butter completely inside.

4.  **Perform the First Turn:** Lightly flour the work surface and the dough package. Carefully roll the dough into a long rectangle, about three times as long as it is wide. Perform a [[Three-Fold]] (or "book fold") by folding the bottom third of the rectangle up over the middle, and the top third down over it, as if folding a letter.

5.  **Chill:** Wrap the folded dough in plastic wrap and chill for 30-45 minutes to allow the gluten to relax and the butter to firm up.

6.  **Complete the Turns:** Repeat the process of rolling, folding (Step 4), and chilling (Step 5) two more times, for a total of three turns. Always rotate the dough 90 degrees before rolling it out for the next turn.

7.  **Final Rest:** After the final turn, wrap the dough and chill for at least 30-45 minutes before using it in a recipe.

## Chef's Notes & Variations
*   **Temperature Control:** The key to successful [[Lamination]] is maintaining a consistent, cool temperature. The [[Butter]] and dough should have a similar plasticity. If the butter is too cold, it will shatter; if it's too warm, it will melt into the dough, ruining the layers.
*   **Number of Turns:** While this recipe calls for three turns, classic puff pastry can have up to six turns. More turns create a finer, more delicate crumb, suitable for pastries like [[Mille-feuille]].
*   **[[Rough Puff Pastry]]**: For a quicker, less technical alternative, try making rough puff. In this method, cold cubes of butter are incorporated directly into the flour, creating a more rustic but still flaky result.
*   **Applications**: This dough is the foundation for countless sweet and savory dishes, including [[Vol-au-vent]], [[Wellington]], [[Palmiers]], and turnovers.

## See Also
*   [[Laminated Dough]]: The foundational category of dough to which puff pastry belongs, characterized by alternating layers of fat and dough.
*   [[Croissant]]: A classic Viennoiserie pastry made using a yeasted laminated dough, a close cousin to puff pastry.
*   [[Rough Puff Pastry]]: A faster, less technically demanding alternative that produces a similar, albeit less refined, flaky texture.
*   [[Détrempe]]: The French term for the simple dough block (flour, water, salt) that forms the base of laminated pastries.
*   [[Beurrage]]: The French term for the butter block that is folded into the détrempe to create the layers.
*   [[Kouign-amann]]: A Breton pastry made with a laminated dough that has sugar incorporated between the layers, resulting in a caramelized crust.

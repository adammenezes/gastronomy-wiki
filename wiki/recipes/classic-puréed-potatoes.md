## Overview
Whether called [[Mashed Potatoes]], [[Whipped Potatoes]], or [[Pommes Purée]], this classic [[comfort food]] is a versatile and beloved accompaniment. Requiring only a few additional ingredients like [[butter]], [[cream]], and seasoning, it serves as an excellent side for [[roasts]], [[stews]], [[grilled steaks]], [[chicken]], or [[fish]]. Beyond its standalone appeal, [[Puréed Potatoes]] also form the foundational base for more elaborate dishes such as [[Duchesse Potatoes]] and [[Twice-Baked Potatoes]].

## Ingredients
- [[Potatoes]]: 1 lb (450 g)
- [[Cream]]/[[Milk]]: 4 oz (120 ml)
- [[Butter]]: 2 oz (60 g)
- [[Salt]]: To Taste
- [[Pepper]]: To Taste

## Method
Step 1 - Prep Ingredients
1.  [[Scrub]] [[potatoes]], remove eyes, sprouts, and green spots; [[peel]] if desired.
2.  Prep whole or [[cut]] (skin-on or off) into large [[dice]] 1 inch (3 cm).
3.  [[Submerge]] in [[cold water]] to prevent discoloration.
4.  Measure the [[cream]] or [[milk]] and place it in a small [[pan]] for heating.
5.  Bring the [[butter]] to room temperature.

Step 2 - Cook the Potatoes
1.  Place the [[potatoes]] in a [[pot]] and add [[cold water]].
2.  Bring the water to a [[simmer]] and gently [[cook]] them until they are tender but not overcooked. Test with a [[fork]] or a [[skewer]].
3.  [[Drain]] through a [[colander]] or [[strainer]] and place back into the [[pot]].
4.  Gently [[heat]] them to allow excess water to [[evaporate]].

Step 3 - Puree and Mix with Cream and Butter
1.  If the [[potatoes]] were cooked in their skin, [[peel]] them first.
2.  Place in a [[food mill]] or a [[potato ricer]] and [[process]] them into a [[mixing bowl]].
3.  Add the [[cream]] and [[butter]] and [[blend]] together to a smooth consistency.
4.  Adjust with [[salt]] and [[pepper]].

## Chef's Notes & Variations
Choosing the right [[potato]] is crucial for the perfect [[purée]]. [[Russet potatoes]] are the best choice due to their high [[starch]] content, while [[Yukon Gold potatoes]] also work well. Avoid [[new potatoes]] like [[red skin potatoes]] or [[fingerling potatoes]], as their higher [[moisture content]] and lower [[starch]] make them less ideal for [[pureeing]].

A common pitfall is using a [[food processor]] or [[blender]] for [[potatoes]]. The high-speed blades tear apart [[starch molecules]], resulting in a [[gummy]] paste rather than light and fluffy potatoes. Instead, opt for manual tools like a [[food mill]] or [[potato ricer]]. A [[grater]] can be used in a pinch. However, this rule doesn't apply to other [[root vegetables]] or [[tuber vegetables]] such as [[carrots]], [[celery root]], [[turnips]], or [[rutabaga]], which can be [[pureed]] in a [[food processor]] without issue.

For maximum flavor, [[cook]] [[potatoes]] whole with the skin on, as [[peeling]] and [[dicing]] can dilute their taste. [[Steaming]] them instead of [[submerging]] in water also enhances flavor. Always start [[potatoes]] in [[cold water]] for even [[cooking]]. [[Simmering]] is preferred over [[boiling]], as aggressive [[cooking]] can cause [[starch gelatinisation]] and disintegration. Avoid adding [[salt]] to the [[cooking]] water, as it can displace [[calcium]] and lead to disintegration. Unlike most [[vegetables]], [[potatoes]] and [[tubers]] should not be [[shocked]] in [[cold water]] after [[cooking]], as this makes them soggy.

Once tender (they should gently give when pierced with a [[fork]] or [[skewer]]), [[drain]] [[potatoes]] immediately using a [[colander]] or [[strainer]]. Return them to the [[pot]] and gently [[dry]] them over low heat for a few minutes to [[evaporate]] excess [[moisture]]. [[Potatoes]] [[puree]] best when warm, so have your [[cream]] and [[butter]] prepped. After [[pureeing]], finish the dish by hand with a [[whisk]] in a [[mixing bowl]] or using a [[standing mixer]] with a [[whip attachment]] to achieve a smooth, [[emulsified]] consistency.

A good ratio for [[puréed potatoes]] is 1 lb (450 g) of [[potatoes]] to 4 oz (120 ml) of [[cream]] or [[milk]] and 2 oz (60 g) of [[butter]]. [[Cream]] provides a richer [[mouthfeel]]. For [[vegan]] options, substitute [[plant-based milk]] (like [[coconut milk]] or [[oat milk]]) and a [[plant-based fat]] such as [[olive oil]] or [[vegan butter]].

This [[technique]] is highly adaptable. Other [[root vegetables]] can be substituted in the same quantity. For specific variations:
*   **[[Potato and Celery Root Puree]]**: Substitute 8 oz (225 g) of [[celery root]] for 8 oz (225 g) of the [[potatoes]]. [[Cook]] them separately due to different [[cooking times]], and [[puree]] the [[celery root]] in a [[food processor]].
*   **[[Potato and Parsnip Puree]]**: Substitute 8 oz (225 g) of [[parsnips]] for 8 oz (225 g) of the [[potatoes]]. [[Cook]] separately and [[puree]] the [[parsnips]] in a [[food processor]].
*   **[[Carrot and Rutabaga Puree]]**: Substitute 12 oz (340 g) of [[carrots]] and 4 oz (120 g) of [[rutabaga]] for the [[potatoes]]. Proceed with the recipe as written.

Enhance your [[puréed potatoes]] with various additions and garnishes: [[bacon]], [[brown butter]], [[Cheddar cheese]], [[Parmesan cheese]], [[Mascarpone cheese]], [[olive oil]], [[sour cream]], [[garlic]], [[chives]], [[parsley]], or [[rosemary]].

## Overview
[[Steamed Salmon]] is a quintessential [[dish]] in [[Healthy Cuisine]], celebrated for its [[simplicity]], quick [[preparation]], and ability to preserve the delicate [[flavor]] and [[nutritional integrity]] of the [[fish]]. This [[method]] of [[Steaming]] gently [[cooks]] the [[Salmon Fillets]], resulting in a [[moist]], [[flaky]] [[texture]] that is both [[light]] and [[satisfying]]. It's a testament to how minimal [[intervention]] can yield maximum [[flavor]], often enhanced by simple [[Aromatics]] like [[Lemon]] and [[Dill]].

## Ingredients
*   2 [[Salmon Fillets]] (about 6 oz / 170g each), [[skin]] on or off
*   1/2 [[Lemon]], [[thinly sliced]]
*   2 sprigs fresh [[Dill]]
*   1/4 teaspoon [[Salt]]
*   1/8 teaspoon freshly ground [[Black Pepper]]
*   [[Water]] for [[Steaming]]

## Method
1.  [[Prepare]] your [[Steamer Pot]] or [[Steamer Basket]]. Fill the [[pot]] with about 1 inch of [[water]], ensuring the [[water level]] is below the bottom of the [[steamer basket]]. Bring the [[water]] to a [[rolling boil]] over [[high heat]].
2.  While the [[water]] heats, [[pat]] the [[Salmon Fillets]] [[dry]] with [[paper towels]]. [[Season]] both sides of the [[fillets]] evenly with [[Salt]] and [[Black Pepper]].
3.  Place a few [[slices]] of [[Lemon]] and a sprig of fresh [[Dill]] on top of each seasoned [[salmon fillet]].
4.  Carefully [[arrange]] the [[salmon fillets]] in the [[Steamer Basket]], ensuring they are in a [[single layer]] and not [[overcrowded]] to allow for even [[Steaming]].
5.  Once the [[water]] is [[boiling]], [[place]] the [[Steamer Basket]] with the [[salmon]] into the [[Steamer Pot]]. [[Cover]] the [[pot]] tightly with a [[lid]].
6.  [[Steam]] the [[salmon]] for 8-12 minutes. The exact [[cooking time]] will depend on the [[thickness]] of your [[fillets]] and your desired level of [[Doneness]]. The [[salmon]] is [[cooked]] when it becomes [[opaque]] throughout and [[flakes]] easily with a [[fork]].
7.  Carefully [[remove]] the [[Steamer Basket]] from the [[pot]]. [[Transfer]] the [[Steamed Salmon]] to [[serving plates]], discarding the [[cooked]] [[lemon slices]] and [[dill sprigs]], or replacing them with [[fresh]] ones for [[garnish]]. [[Serve]] immediately.

## Chef's Notes & Variations
Achieving the perfect [[Doneness]] is key in [[Fish Cookery]]; [[salmon]] should be [[moist]] and [[tender]], not [[dry]]. [[Overcooking]] is a common [[mistake]] that can lead to a [[tough]], [[chalky]] [[texture]]. A good [[indicator]] is when the [[salmon]] just begins to [[Flake]] easily. For added [[flavor]], consider a quick [[Marinade]] for the [[salmon]] before [[steaming]], using [[ingredients]] like [[Soy Sauce]], [[Ginger]], or [[Garlic]]. You can also [[experiment]] with other [[Aromatics]] in the [[steamer water]] itself, such as [[Lemongrass]], [[Bay Leaves]], or [[White Wine]], to [[infuse]] the [[fish]] with [[subtle notes]]. This [[dish]] pairs wonderfully with [[Steamed Vegetables]] like [[Asparagus]] or [[Broccoli]], or a light [[grain]] like [[Quinoa]] or Brown [[Rice]] for a complete and balanced [[meal]].

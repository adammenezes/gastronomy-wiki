## Overview
This recipe outlines the fundamental process for creating a classic lean dough, suitable for shaping into a traditional [[Baguette]] or a rustic [[Boule]]. Originating from [[French Cuisine]], this dough's character is developed not through enrichment, but through careful technique, including multiple stages of [[Bulk Fermentation]] and [[Folding]]. The process builds a strong [[Gluten]] network, resulting in the chewy texture and open crumb structure characteristic of artisanal bread.

## Ingredients
*   [[Flour]]
*   [[Yeast]]
*   [[Water]]
*   [[Salt]]
*   [[Egg Wash]] (optional)

## Method
1.  **Mix the Dough:** In a large [[Mixing Bowl]] or the bowl of a stand mixer, combine the [[Water]] and [[Salt]]. In a separate bowl, combine the [[Flour]] and [[Yeast]]. Add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients. Using a [[Dough Hook]] attachment, mix on low to medium speed for 5 minutes. The goal is to develop the [[Gluten]] network until the dough is smooth and elastic. Adjust with a small amount of water if too dry or flour if too wet to achieve the correct [[Hydration]].

2.  **Bulk Ferment:** Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface or place it in a lightly oiled bowl. This primary rising stage is known as [[Bulk Fermentation]]. Cover with a damp cloth and let it ferment until doubled in size. Gently perform a [[Folding]] on the dough, turn it over, and allow it to ferment a second time until it doubles again. Fold the dough a final time and let it rest for an additional 15 minutes.

3.  **Divide and Preshape:** Perform the [[Dividing]] of the dough into 1 lb (450 g) pieces. Gently shape each piece into a round, a technique known as [[Preshaping]]. Cover the rounds and allow them to relax for 15-20 minutes. This rest period allows the [[Gluten]] to relax, making the final shaping easier.

4. **Shape and Proof:** Proceed with the final [[Shaping]], forming the dough into long [[Baguette]] shapes or round [[Boule]] loaves. Place the shaped loaves on a baking sheet or in a proofing basket. The final rise, or [[Proofing]], should last for 1 to 1.5 hours, or until the dough is puffy and has increased significantly in volume.

5.  **Score and Bake:** Preheat an [[Oven]] to 450°F (225°C). Just before baking, perform the [[Scoring]] by making decisive cuts on the surface of the dough with a sharp blade or lame. This controls the [[Oven Spring]], or the final burst of rising during baking. If desired, brush the surface with an [[Egg Wash]] for a golden, shiny crust. Bake for 25-30 minutes until deep golden brown and hollow-sounding when tapped on the bottom.

## Chef's Notes & Variations
*   **Steam Injection:** For a crispier, more authentic crust, it is crucial to introduce steam into the [[Oven]] for the first 10-15 minutes of baking. This can be achieved by placing a [[Cast Iron Pan]] with hot water on a lower rack.
*   **Flour Choice:** Using a high-protein [[Bread Flour]] will yield a chewier crumb due to stronger [[Gluten]] development. An all-purpose [[Flour]] will also work but may result in a slightly softer texture.
*   **Cold Fermentation:** For more complex flavor development, the [[Bulk Fermentation]] stage can be done slowly in a refrigerator for 12-24 hours. This [[Cold Fermentation]] technique slows down the [[Yeast]] activity, allowing more nuanced flavors to emerge.
*   **Checking for Doneness:** Besides color, you can check for doneness using an instant-read [[Thermometer]]. The internal temperature of the bread should be between 200-210°F (93-99°C).

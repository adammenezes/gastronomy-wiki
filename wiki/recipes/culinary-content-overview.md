## Overview
[[Steamed Salmon]] is a classic, healthy, and incredibly simple dish that highlights the natural flavors of fresh [[Salmon]]. This [[Steaming|cooking method]] involves gently cooking the fish with [[Steam]], resulting in a moist, flaky texture and preserving its delicate taste. It's a staple in many [[Healthy Cooking]] repertoires and is often served with [[Vegetables]] or [[Grains]].

## Ingredients
*   2 (6 oz / 170g) [[Salmon]] fillets, skin on or off
*   1/2 [[Lemon]], thinly sliced
*   2 sprigs fresh [[Dill]]
*   1/4 teaspoon [[Salt]]
*   1/8 teaspoon freshly ground [[Black Pepper]]
*   (Optional) 1 tablespoon [[White Wine]] or [[Vegetable Broth]]

## Method
1.  **Prepare the [[Steamer]]:** Fill the bottom of a [[Steamer Pot]] or [[Bamboo Steamer]] with about 1-2 inches of water. Bring the water to a boil over medium-high heat.
2.  **Season the [[Salmon]]:** Pat the [[Salmon]] fillets dry with paper towels. Season both sides with [[Salt]] and [[Black Pepper]]. This [[Seasoning]] step is crucial for flavor.
3.  **Arrange for [[Steaming]]:** Place the [[Salmon]] fillets in the [[Steamer Basket]]. Top each fillet with a few slices of [[Lemon]] and a sprig of fresh [[Dill]]. If using, pour the [[White Wine]] or [[Vegetable Broth]] over the [[Salmon]].
4.  **[[Steam]] the [[Salmon]]:** Once the water is boiling, place the [[Steamer Basket]] over the pot, ensuring the water does not touch the bottom of the basket. Cover tightly with a lid. Reduce heat to medium and [[Steam]] for 8-12 minutes, or until the [[Salmon]] is opaque throughout and flakes easily with a fork. The exact [[Cooking Time]] will depend on the thickness of the fillets.
5.  **Serve:** Carefully remove the [[Steamed Salmon]] from the basket. Serve immediately with your choice of side dishes, such as [[Steamed Vegetables]] or [[Quinoa]].

## Chef's Notes & Variations
*   **Don't Overcook:** The key to perfectly [[Steamed Salmon]] is to avoid [[Overcooking]]. [[Salmon]] is best when it's just cooked through, remaining moist and tender. Use an [[Instant-Read Thermometer]] to check for an internal temperature of 145°F (63°C).
*   **Flavor Variations:** Experiment with other aromatics like thin slices of fresh [[Ginger]], minced [[Garlic]] cloves, chopped [[Scallions]], or different fresh [[Herbs]] such as [[Parsley]] or [[Cilantro]]. A splash of [[Soy Sauce]] or [[Sesame Oil]] can add an [[Asian Cuisine]] twist.
*   **Serving Suggestions:** This dish pairs wonderfully with a light [[Vinaigrette]], a squeeze of fresh [[Lemon Juice]], or a dollop of [[Yogurt Sauce]]. Consider serving it alongside [[Asparagus]], [[Broccoli]], [[Brown Rice]], or a simple [[Green Salad]].
*   **Equipment:** While a traditional [[Steamer Pot]] or [[Bamboo Steamer]] is ideal, you can also use a metal [[Steamer Insert]] in any large pot with a tight-fitting lid.

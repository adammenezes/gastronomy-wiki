## Overview
Danish Dough is a rich, yeasted [[Viennoiserie]] pastry dough originating from [[Danish Cuisine]]. It is a type of [[Laminated Dough]], characterized by dozens of thin, alternating layers of dough and butter. This structure, created through a meticulous process of [[Folding]] and rolling, results in an exceptionally flaky, tender, and buttery texture when baked. It serves as the foundation for many classic pastries, including the eponymous [[Danish Pastry]].

## Ingredients
*   [[Milk]]
*   [[Yeast]]
*   5 oz. / 125 g [[Butter]], for the dough (détrempe)
*   Additional [[Butter]] block for lamination (beurrage)
*   [[Sugar]]
*   [[Salt]]
*   [[Cardamom]], ground
*   [[Egg]]s
*   [[Flour]]
*   [[Egg Wash]]

## Method
1.  **Prepare the Détrempe (Dough Base):** [[Scald]] the [[Milk]], then allow it to cool to a lukewarm temperature. Dissolve the [[Yeast]] in the warm milk.
2.  In the bowl of a [[Stand Mixer]] fitted with a [[Paddle Attachment]], cream together 5 oz. / 125 g of [[Butter]] with the [[Sugar]], [[Salt]], and [[Cardamom]] until smooth. Beat in the [[Egg]]s one at a time.
3.  Add the milk-yeast mixture and the [[Flour]] to the bowl. Switch to a [[Dough Hook]] and mix on medium speed for 3-4 minutes until a cohesive dough forms. Do not over-mix.
4.  Turn the dough out onto a [[Parchment Paper]]-lined sheet pan and shape it into a flat rectangle. Place it in a [[Cooler]] or refrigerator for 30-45 minutes. This initial [[Retarding]] chills the dough, making it easier to handle and controlling the yeast activity.
5.  **Laminate the Dough:** On a lightly floured surface, roll the chilled dough into a larger rectangle. Place your prepared butter block (beurrage) onto the dough and perform the [[Lock-in]] using your preferred [[Folding]] method (such as a single/book fold or an envelope/letter fold).
6.  Perform the first [[Turning|turn]] by rolling the dough out into a long rectangle and folding it into thirds. Wrap the dough and chill for 30-45 minutes. Repeat this process of rolling, turning, and chilling two more times for a total of three turns.
7.  **Shape and Proof:** After the final chill, roll the dough out to your desired thickness. Cut and shape it as needed for your intended pastry.
8.  Arrange the shaped pastries on a baking sheet, brush with [[Egg Wash]], and allow them to [[Proofing|proof]] in a warm, humid environment until they have nearly doubled in size. Use minimal steam to prevent the butter from melting out.
9.  **Bake:** Bake in a preheated oven at 375°F / 190°C until golden brown and cooked through.

## Chef's Notes & Variations
*   **Temperature is Key:** The success of any [[Laminated Dough]] relies on maintaining the correct temperature. Both the dough (détrempe) and the butter block (beurrage) should be cold but pliable. If the butter is too hard, it will shatter; if it's too soft, it will merge with the dough, and you will lose the distinct layers.
*   **Traditional Spice:** [[Cardamom]] is a classic spice in [[Scandinavian Cuisine]] and gives this dough its characteristic aroma. For a different profile, you could substitute it with [[Cinnamon]] or [[Nutmeg]].
*   **Fillings:** This dough is a versatile base for [[Danish Pastry]]. Classic fillings include [[Cream Cheese]], almond paste ([[Frangipane]]), or fruit preserves.
*   **Avoid Over-Proofing:** Over-proofing can cause the delicate butter layers to melt and leak out during [[Baking|baking]], resulting in a greasy, heavy pastry rather than a light and flaky one.

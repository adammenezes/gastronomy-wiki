## Overview
Blitz Puff Pastry, also known as rough [[puff pastry]], is a streamlined [[method]] for creating a [[Laminated Dough]]. Originating as a quicker alternative within [[French Cuisine]] to [[classic]] [[Puff Pastry]], this [[technique]] incorporates cold [[butter]] [[chunks]] directly into the [[dough]] rather than enclosing a [[single]] [[large]] [[block]]. The subsequent [[rolling]] and [[folding]] creates hundreds of [[flaky]] [[layers]], though with a slightly more [[rustic]] [[texture]] than its [[traditional]] counterpart.

## Ingredients
*   [[Flour]]
*   [[Salt]]
*   Cold [[Butter]], cubed
*   Ice-cold [[Water]]

## Method
1.  **Combine and Mix:** In the [[bowl]] of a [[Mixer]], [[combine]] the [[Flour]], [[Salt]], and cold [[Butter]] [[chunks]]. [[Mix]] briefly to lightly [[coat]] the [[butter]] with [[flour]]. Add the ice-cold [[Water]] and [[mix]] on [[low]] [[speed]] with a [[Dough Hook Attachment]] just until the [[flour]] is [[hydrated]] and the [[dough]] comes together into a [[shaggy]], [[sticky]] [[mass]]. Do not [[overmix]]; [[visible]] [[chunks]] of [[butter]] should remain.
2.  **Initial Chill:** [[Turn]] the [[dough]] out, gently [[form]] it into a [[rough]] [[square]], and [[wrap]] it tightly in [[Plastic Wrap]]. [[Chill]] in the [[refrigerator]] for at least 30 minutes to [[firm]] up the [[butter]] and [[relax]] the [[gluten]].
3.  **First Fold:** Place the [[chilled]] [[dough]] on a lightly [[floured]] [[surface]]. [[Roll]] it out into a [[rectangle]] approximately ½ [[inch]] (1 [[cm]]) [[thick]]. [[Perform]] a [[Three-Fold]] or a [[Four-Fold]] (book [[fold]]) by [[folding]] the [[dough]] over on itself like a [[letter]].
4.  **Repeat Folds & Turns:** [[Wrap]] the [[dough]] and [[chill]] for another 30 minutes. [[Repeat]] the [[process]] of [[rolling]] and [[folding]] three more times. After each [[fold]], [[turn]] the [[dough]] 90° before [[rolling]] again to ensure [[even]] [[layering]]. [[Chill]] the [[dough]] for 30 minutes between each [[turn]].
5.  **Final Chill:** After the final [[fold]], [[wrap]] the [[dough]] and [[chill]] for at least 1 [[hour]] before [[using]] it in your desired [[application]].

## Chef's Notes & Variations
*   **Keep It Cold:** The [[key]] to [[successful]] Blitz [[Puff Pastry]] is [[temperature control]]. The [[Butter]] must remain [[solid]] and [[distinct]] from the [[dough]]. If it [[melts]], the [[layers]] will be [[lost]]. Use ice-cold [[water]] and [[chill]] the [[dough]] thoroughly between [[folds]].
*   **Flour Management:** [[Dust]] your [[work surface]] and [[dough]] with [[Flour]] as needed to [[prevent]] [[sticking]], but be sure to [[brush]] off any [[excess]] with a [[pastry brush]] before [[folding]]. [[Excess]] [[flour]] can make the final [[pastry]] [[tough]].
*   **Comparison to Classic Puff:** While this [[method]] is significantly [[faster]], it [[yields]] a [[pastry]] with a slightly less [[uniform]] and [[dramatic]] [[rise]] compared to [[traditional]] [[Puff Pastry]]. This makes it [[excellent]] for [[applications]] like [[turnovers]], [[sausage rolls]], or [[tart bases]].
*   **Resting:** Allowing the [[dough]] to [[rest]] during the [[chilling]] periods is [[crucial]] for [[relaxing]] the [[Gluten]], which makes the [[dough]] [[easier]] to [[roll]] out for [[subsequent]] [[folds]].

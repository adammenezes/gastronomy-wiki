## Overview
Avocado Prep refers to the set of techniques for halving, pitting, peeling, and cutting an [[Avocado]] for culinary use. Proper preparation is crucial for safety, maximising yield, and achieving the desired texture for dishes ranging from [[Guacamole]] and [[Avocado Toast]] to a [[Cobb Salad]]. The primary challenge in avocado preparation is managing its rapid browning, a process known as [[Enzymatic Browning]], once the flesh is exposed to air.

## The Science
The browning of avocado flesh is a classic example of [[Enzymatic Browning]]. When an [[Avocado]] is cut, its cell walls are ruptured, exposing an enzyme called [[Polyphenol Oxidase]] (PPO) to oxygen in the air. This enzyme rapidly oxidises phenolic compounds naturally present in the fruit, converting them into quinones. These quinones then polymerize, forming the brown-coloured pigments known as melanins. This reaction can be slowed significantly by introducing an acid, such as [[Lemon Juice]] or [[Lime Juice]]. The [[Ascorbic Acid]] (Vitamin C) in these juices acts as an antioxidant, and the lower [[pH]] environment inhibits the activity of the PPO enzyme.

## How to Do It
1.  **Halve the Avocado:** Place the [[Avocado]] on a stable [[Cutting Board]]. Using a sharp [[Chef's Knife]], carefully slice it lengthwise around the central pit, starting from the stem and rotating the fruit until you've made a complete circle.
2.  **Separate the Halves:** Gently twist the two halves in opposite directions. They should separate easily, with the pit remaining in one side.
3.  **Remove the Pit:** Place the avocado half with the pit on the [[Cutting Board]] (never in your hand). Firmly but carefully tap the blade of your [[Chef's Knife]] into the pit so it lodges. Twist the knife to dislodge the pit and lift it out. To remove the pit from the knife, press the sides of the pit against the edge of a compost bin or trash can to release it.
4.  **Scoop or Peel:** Using a large [[Spoon]], slide it between the skin and the flesh and scoop out the meat in one piece. Alternatively, if the avocado is very ripe, you can quarter the halves and peel the skin away with your fingers.
5.  **Process the Flesh:** Place the flesh flat-side down on the cutting board. From here, you can proceed with [[Slicing]], [[Dicing]], or [[Mashing]] with a [[Fork]].
6.  **Prevent Browning:** If not using immediately, toss the prepared avocado with a small amount of [[Lemon Juice]] or [[Lime Juice]].

## Common Mistakes
*   **"Avocado Hand":** This common kitchen injury occurs when trying to remove the pit while holding the avocado half in your palm. The knife can slip and cause a serious cut. **Fix:** Always place the avocado half on a [[Cutting Board]] before striking the pit with a [[Chef's Knife]].
*   **Bruising the Flesh:** Using a dull knife or being too aggressive when scooping with a [[Spoon]] can bruise the delicate flesh, leading to discolouration and a mushy texture. **Fix:** Use a sharp knife for clean cuts and a gentle hand when scooping.
*   **Letting it Brown:** Exposing the cut avocado to air for even a short period will initiate [[Oxidation]]. **Fix:** Prepare the avocado just before serving, or immediately treat it with an acid like [[Lime Juice]] and cover it with plastic wrap pressed directly against the surface.
*   **Using an Unripe Avocado:** The flesh will be hard, lack flavour, and be difficult to mash or even slice cleanly. **Fix:** Test for ripeness by gently pressing near the stem; a ripe avocado will yield to gentle pressure.

## When to Use It
The final form of the prepared avocado depends on its intended use.
*   **Mashed:** Ideal for creating smooth, creamy bases for dips like [[Guacamole]], spreads for [[Avocado Toast]], or as a fat substitute in baked goods.
*   **Diced:** Perfect for adding texture and richness to salads like the [[Cobb Salad]], salsas, or as a garnish for [[Tacos]] and [[Chili]].
*   **Sliced:** Best for layering in sandwiches, burgers, and [[Sushi]] rolls like the [[California Roll]]. Thin slices can also be fanned out for an elegant presentation.
*   **Halved:** The scooped-out halves can be grilled or used as edible bowls, often filled with a baked [[Egg]] or [[Tuna Salad]].

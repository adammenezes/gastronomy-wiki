## Overview
[[Citrus Supreming]] is a classic [[French Cuisine|French]] [[Knife Skills|knife technique]] used to segment [[citrus fruits]] by removing the [[peel]], [[Pith]], and [[membrane]]. The result is a collection of pure, skinless [[fruit segments]], known as *[[suprêmes]]*. This method is prized for its ability to produce an elegant [[presentation]] and a [[cleaner]], more intense [[citrus]] [[flavour]] by eliminating the [[bitter]] components. [[Suprêmes]] are commonly used in [[dishes]] like [[Fruit Salad]], [[desserts]], and as a sophisticated [[Garnish]] for both [[sweet]] and [[savory]] [[plates]], featuring [[fruits]] like [[Orange]], [[Grapefruit]], [[Lemon]], and [[Lime]].

## The Science
The primary goal of [[supreming]] is to isolate the [[juicy]] [[flesh]] of the [[citrus fruit]] from its [[bitter]] components. The [[white]], [[spongy]] [[layer]] between the [[outer peel]] ([[Zest]]) and the [[fruit's segments]] is called the [[Pith]], or [[albedo]]. This [[layer]], along with the [[membranes]] separating each [[segment]], contains [[bitter-tasting]] [[flavonoid]] [[compounds]], most notably [[Naringin]] in [[Grapefruit]]. By carefully [[cutting away]] the [[peel]], [[pith]], and [[membrane]], the [[technique]] ensures that only the [[sweet]] and [[acidic]] [[juice-filled vesicles]] of the [[fruit segments]] remain, resulting in a superior [[taste]] and [[texture]].

## How to Do It
1.  **Preparation**: Begin with a stable [[Cutting Board]] and a [[sharp]] [[Paring Knife]] or a small, nimble [[Chef's Knife]].
2.  **Create a Base**: [[Slice off]] the [[top]] and [[bottom]] of the [[Citrus Fruit]] to create two [[flat]] [[surfaces]]. This prevents the [[fruit]] from [[rolling]] during [[peeling]].
3.  **Remove Peel and Pith**: [[Stand]] the [[fruit]] on one of its [[flat]] [[ends]]. Following the natural [[curve]] of the [[fruit]], [[slice downwards]] from [[top]] to [[bottom]], [[cutting away]] both the [[outer peel]] and all of the [[white Pith]]. [[Rotate]] the [[fruit]] and [[repeat]] until it is completely [[peeled]].
4.  **Segment the Fruit**: [[Hold]] the [[peeled fruit]] securely in your [[non-dominant hand]] over a [[bowl]] to catch any [[juice]]. Identify the [[thin membranes]] that [[separate]] the [[segments]].
5.  **Release the Suprême**: Make a [[precise cut]] with your [[Paring Knife]] just inside one of the [[membranes]], [[slicing]] from the [[outside]] towards the [[core]]. Make a [[second]], similar [[cut]] on the other side of the same [[segment]], against the neighbouring [[membrane]]. The [[segment]], or *[[suprême]]*, should [[release]] and [[fall]] into the [[bowl]].
6.  **Finish**: [[Repeat]] this [[process]] for all remaining [[segments]]. Once finished, [[squeeze]] the leftover [[membrane]] "skeleton" over the [[bowl]] to [[extract]] all remaining [[juice]].

## Common Mistakes
*   **Using a Dull Knife**: A [[dull blade]] will [[tear]] the [[delicate]] [[fruit flesh]] and make it difficult to [[cleanly separate]] the [[segments]] from the [[membrane]]. A [[sharp Paring Knife]] is [[crucial]] for [[precision]].
*   **Leaving Pith Behind**: Any [[residual white Pith]] will impart a noticeable [[bitterness]]. Be [[meticulous]] about [[removing]] all of it during the [[peeling]] [[stage]].
*   **Wasting Fruit**: [[Cutting]] too [[deeply]] into the [[flesh]] while [[removing]] the [[peel]] results in significant [[waste]]. The [[key]] is to [[follow]] the [[fruit's curvature]] as [[closely]] as possible.
*   **Working on an Unstable Surface**: Failing to [[slice off]] the [[top]] and [[bottom]] of the [[Citrus Fruit]] creates a [[wobbly]], [[unsafe]] [[base]] for [[peeling]] and increases the [[risk]] of the [[knife slipping]].

## When to Use It
This [[technique]] is ideal for [[applications]] where the [[texture]] and [[pure flavour]] of [[citrus]] are [[paramount]].
*   **Salads**: Elevates a simple [[Fruit Salad]] or a [[green salad]] by providing [[tender]], [[juicy]] [[bursts]] of [[citrus]] without any [[chewy membrane]].
*   **Desserts**: Perfect for [[topping]] a [[Panna Cotta]], [[layering]] in a [[Citrus Tart]], or [[folding]] into a [[mousse]] for a [[clean flavour]] and [[beautiful appearance]].
*   **Savory Dishes**: Use as a [[Garnish]] for [[Pan-Seared Scallops]], [[Duck Breast]], or in a [[fish dish]] like [[Ceviche]] to add a [[bright]], [[acidic]] [[counterpoint]].
*   **Sauces and Dressings**: The [[collected juice]] and [[segments]] can be [[incorporated]] directly into a [[Vinaigrette]] for a [[fresh]], [[vibrant dressing]].

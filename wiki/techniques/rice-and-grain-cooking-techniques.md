## Overview
[[Cooking Rice & Grains]] encompasses a variety of methods for preparing [[Rice]] and other [[Grains]], which are staple foods globally. As the second most popular grain after [[Corn]], [[Rice]] is incredibly versatile, appearing in [[Soups]], [[Salads]], entrees, side dishes, and [[Desserts]]. Many classic preparations, such as [[Pilaf]] and [[Risotto]], can be adapted for other grains like [[Barley]], [[Quinoa]], and even [[Orzo]] [[Pasta]]. These techniques are fundamental in [[Culinary Arts]], providing cost-effective and adaptable options for diverse menus.

## The Science
The primary scientific principle behind [[Cooking Rice & Grains]] is [[Starch Gelatinisation]]. When [[Grains]] are heated in [[Liquid]], the [[Starch]] granules absorb [[Water]] and swell, eventually bursting and releasing [[Amylose]] and [[Amylopectin]]. This process causes the grains to soften and, in some cases, thicken the surrounding liquid, as seen in [[Porridge]] or [[Risotto]]. The goal is often to achieve an [[Al Dente]] texture, where the grains are tender but still retain a slight firmness. Different grain varieties have varying [[Starch]] compositions, influencing their cooking times and ideal liquid ratios. For example, [[Short Grain Rice]] varieties like [[Arborio]] are high in [[Amylopectin]], contributing to their creamy texture when cooked using methods like [[Risotto]].

## How to Do It

### Basic Preparations

#### Boiled
This method is similar to [[Pasta]] preparation and suits most [[Grains]].
1.  Use a ratio of one gallon (four liters) of [[Water]] to one pound (450 grams) of [[Grains]].
2.  [[Salt]] the [[Water]], bring it to a full boil in a large [[Pot]], and add the [[Rice]].
3.  Reduce heat to a gentle simmer and stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
4.  Cook until the grains are [[Al Dente]] – tender but with a firm texture.
5.  Strain the cooked grains using a [[Strainer]] and rinse with [[Water]].
6.  Use for [[Salads]], [[Soups]], or as a [[Par-cooking]] stage for later finishing.

#### Steamed & Simmered
Essentially the same process, where [[Liquid]] is absorbed by the grains.
1.  Combine the measured [[Liquid]] with the [[Grains]] in a [[Pot]].
2.  The grains may be rinsed or soaked beforehand, or even [[Blanched]] in several changes of [[Water]] to remove bitterness or impurities.
3.  Cover the pot and gently bring the mixture to a simmer.
4.  Cook until all liquid is absorbed and grains are tender.

#### Porridge, Polenta & Grits
These preparations involve boiling [[Oats]], [[Rice]], [[Wheat]], [[Barley]], or [[Corn]] to create a thick, creamy meal.
1.  [[Grains]] (rolled, steel-cut, or ground) are simmered in [[Liquid]] (e.g., [[Water]], [[Stock]], [[Milk]]) until they swell and thicken the mixture.
2.  For sweet preparations like [[Oatmeal]], add [[Sugar]], [[Honey]], [[Maple Syrup]], [[Dried Fruits]], or [[Nuts]].
3.  For savory dishes like [[Grits]] and [[Polenta]], incorporate [[Onions]], [[Garlic]], other [[Aromatics]], [[Cheese]], [[Bacon]], or [[Eggs]].

### Advanced Preparations

#### Pilaf
A [[Braising]] method for [[Rice]] or other [[Grains]].
1.  Begin by [[Sweating Aromatics]] such as [[Onions]], [[Carrots]], [[Celery]], and [[Garlic]] in [[Fat]] in a [[Pot]].
2.  Add the [[Rice]] (e.g., [[Long Grain Rice]] like [[Basmati]] or [[Carola]]) and [[Parch]] or toast it in the [[Fat]] to develop color and flavor.
3.  Add the [[Liquid]] (e.g., [[Stock]]) and seasonings.
4.  Cover the pot and cook, either on the stovetop or in an [[Oven]], until the liquid is absorbed.
5.  Garnish with [[Meats]], [[Cheeses]], [[Herbs]], [[Spices]], [[Lemon Zest]], [[Dried Fruits]], or [[Toasted Nuts]] after cooking. Add fresh [[Herbs]] at service.
    *   **Variations:** Experiment with different [[Grains]], [[Aromatics]] (e.g., [[Matignon]], [[Mirepoix]], [[Cuban Sofrito]], [[Cajun Trinity]], [[Italian Battuto]]), [[Proteins]] (e.g., [[Bacon]], [[Salt Pork]], [[Chicken]], [[Lamb]], [[Seafood]], [[Legumes]]), [[Herbs]] and [[Spices]] (e.g., [[Curry Powder]], [[Garam Masala]], [[Achiote Powder]]), and [[Liquids]] (e.g., [[Chicken Stock]], [[Vegetable Stock]], [[Tomato Juice]], [[Coconut Milk]]).

#### Risotto
An [[Italian Cuisine]] method for [[Short Grain Rice]].
1.  Start by [[Sweating Aromatics]] like [[Garlic]] and [[Onions]] (or [[Shallots]], [[Leeks]]) in [[Fat]] in a [[Pot]]. Add other [[Vegetables]] like [[Mushrooms]], [[Celery]], or [[Carrots]].
2.  Add [[Short Grain Rice]] (e.g., [[Arborio]], [[Vialone Nano]], [[Carnaroli]]) and toast briefly.
3.  [[Deglaze]] the pot with [[White Wine]].
4.  Gradually add [[Stock]] (e.g., [[Chicken Stock]], [[Vegetable Stock]]) one [[Ladle]] at a time, stirring gently to "rearrange" the [[Rice]] until the liquid evaporates.
5.  Continue adding liquid until the [[Rice]] is cooked through but still has a distinct [[Al Dente]] firmness.
6.  Finish the dish by stirring in [[Butter]] and [[Cheese]] for a creamy, savory texture.
    *   **Foodservice Prep:** [[Par-cook]] the [[Rice]] until slightly underdone, cool on a [[Sheet Pan]], and finish to order with additional [[Stock]], [[Cheese]], [[Butter]], and garnishes.
    *   **Variations:** Substitute [[Grains]] like [[Barley]], [[Brown Rice]], [[Orzo]], [[Farro]], or [[Thai Black Rice]]. Vary [[Aromatics]] (e.g., [[Italian Soffritto]], [[Battuto]], [[Beets]], [[Celery Root]], [[Butternut Squash]]). Add [[Proteins]] (e.g., [[Bacon]], [[Lamb]], [[Shrimp]], [[Scallops]], [[Mussels]], [[Legumes]]). Incorporate [[Spices]] (e.g., [[Saffron]], [[Cajun Spices]], [[Curry Powder]]) or [[Herbs]] (e.g., [[Sage]], [[Chives]], [[Basil Pesto]]). Use different [[Liquids]] (e.g., [[Red Wine]]).

### Other Braised Rice Dishes

#### Paella
A [[Spanish Cuisine]] dish from [[Valencia]].
1.  Sauté [[Meats]] (e.g., [[Chorizo]], [[Chicken]], [[Rabbit]]) in a [[Paella Pan]].
2.  Add [[Onions]], [[Garlic]], [[Peppers]], [[Tomatoes]], and [[Paprika]].
3.  Combine [[Broth]] with [[Saffron]] and add to the pan along with [[Short Grain Rice]] (e.g., [[Calasparra]], [[Bomba]]).
4.  Cook until liquid is absorbed and a crust forms on the bottom.
5.  Garnish with [[Seafood]] (e.g., [[Shrimp]], [[Lobsters]]).

#### Jambalaya
A [[Louisiana Cuisine]] one-pot dish, influenced by [[Spanish Paella]] and [[French Cuisine]].
1.  Brown [[Meats]] (e.g., [[Sausage]], [[Chicken]], [[Pork]], [[Alligator]], [[Duck]], [[Turtle]], [[Venison]]) in [[Fat]] until a deep color develops and bits stick to the bottom of the [[Pot]].
2.  Add the [[Mirepoix]] "trinity" of [[Onions]], [[Peppers]], and [[Celery]], along with [[Garlic]], and caramelize with the meats.
3.  For [[Creole Jambalaya]], add [[Tomatoes]]. Season with [[Salt]], [[Pepper]], [[Thyme]], and [[Cayenne]].
4.  Combine [[Liquid]] with the meats and [[Mirepoix]], bring to a simmer, and add the [[Rice]].
5.  Cover and simmer, stirring occasionally, until the [[Rice]] is tender.

#### Fried Rice
An [[Asian Cuisine]] preparation.
1.  [[Stir-fry]] cold, day-old [[Long Grain Rice]] (e.g., [[Jasmine]]) in a [[Wok]] or large [[Pan]].
2.  Add other ingredients such as [[Eggs]], [[Meat]] (e.g., [[Chicken]], [[Beef]], [[Cured Pork]]), [[Seafood]] (e.g., [[Shrimp]], [[Lobster]]), [[Vegetables]] (e.g., [[Carrots]], [[Broccoli]], [[Bean Sprouts]], [[Celery]], [[Peas]], [[Corn]]), [[Mushrooms]], [[Spices]], and [[Peppers]].
3.  Season with [[Garlic]], [[Ginger]], and [[Soy Sauce]].

#### Bibimbap
A [[Korean Cuisine]] mixed [[Rice]] dish.
1.  Combine cooked [[Rice]] with sautéed [[Vegetables]] (e.g., [[Bean Sprouts]], [[Cucumber]], [[Mushrooms]], [[Spinach]], [[Zucchini]]), [[Chili Pepper Paste]], and [[Soy Sauce]].
2.  Garnish with a [[Fried Egg]] and sliced [[Meats]] (e.g., [[Chicken]], [[Fish]], [[Tofu]]).
3.  Stir together just before eating.

#### Biryani
An [[Indian Cuisine]] [[Rice]] dish.
1.  Cook [[Rice]] with [[Lamb]], [[Goat]], or [[Chicken]].
2.  Season with [[Nutmeg]], [[Cinnamon]], [[Cloves]], [[Cardamom]], [[Coriander]], [[Mint]], [[Ginger]], [[Onions]], and [[Garlic]].
3.  Serve with [[Chutney]] and [[Raita]].

#### Congee
A [[Japanese Cuisine]] breakfast dish.
1.  Prepare [[Long Grain Rice]] as a [[Porridge]].
2.  Garnish with [[Meats]], [[Fish]], [[Vegetables]], and [[Herbs]].
3.  Sweet versions incorporate [[Sugar]], [[Dried Fruits]], and [[Nuts]].

## Common Mistakes
*   **Over-stirring Risotto:** While stirring is essential, excessive stirring can break down the [[Short Grain Rice]], resulting in a gummy texture rather than creamy.
*   **Not Rinsing Grains:** For some preparations, not rinsing [[Grains]] can leave excess [[Starch]] on the surface, leading to stickiness, or fail to remove bitterness or impurities.
*   **Using Fresh Rice for Fried Rice:** [[Cold Rice]], preferably day-old, is crucial for [[Fried Rice]] as it is drier and less likely to clump, allowing for better separation and texture.
*   **Incorrect Liquid Ratios:** Using too much or too little [[Liquid]] for boiled or steamed [[Grains]] can result in mushy or undercooked [[Rice]].
*   **Skipping the Toasting/Parching Step for Pilaf:** Toasting the [[Rice]] in [[Fat]] before adding liquid in [[Pilaf]] adds a nutty flavor and helps the grains maintain their individual shape.
*   **Not Cooking to [[Al Dente]]:** Overcooking can lead to mushy grains, while undercooking leaves them hard and unpalatable.

## When to Use It
[[Cooking Rice & Grains]] techniques are fundamental for a vast array of dishes:
*   **Boiled/Steamed Grains:** Ideal for [[Salads]], [[Soups]], or as a neutral side dish. Also used as a [[Par-cooking]] step for other preparations.
*   **Porridge/Polenta/Grits:** Perfect for hearty breakfast cereals or comforting side dishes, adaptable to sweet or savory profiles.
*   **Pilaf:** When you want a flavorful, aromatic [[Rice]] or grain dish that can stand alone or accompany [[Meats]] and [[Vegetables]].
*   **Risotto:** For a creamy, rich, and indulgent [[Rice]] dish, often served as a first course or a main.
*   **Paella, Jambalaya, Fried Rice, Bibimbap, Biryani, Congee:** These are specific, iconic dishes from various [[Cuisines]] that rely on their unique grain cooking methods to define their character.
*   **Ingredients:** Applicable to virtually all [[Grains]], including [[Rice]] (long, medium, short grain), [[Barley]], [[Quinoa]], [[Oats]], [[Corn]], [[Farro]], [[Orzo]], and [[Wheat]].

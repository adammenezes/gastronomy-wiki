## Overview
Fruit preparation encompasses the range of techniques used to clean, cut, and process fruits for cooking or raw consumption. This foundational skill set is essential for everything from a simple [[Fruit Salad]] to complex pastries like [[Tarts]] and pies. Proper prep ensures fruits are safe to eat, visually appealing, and have the correct texture. It involves tasks like washing, peeling, coring, seeding, and cutting into specific shapes. While we culinarily associate the term with sweet items like [[Apples]], [[Berries]], and [[Citrus]], botanically it also includes items like [[Tomato]], [[Squash]], and [[Avocado]].

## The Science
The most significant chemical reaction in fruit prep is [[Enzymatic Browning]]. When fruits like [[Apples]], [[Pears]], or [[Bananas]] are cut, their cell walls are ruptured, exposing an enzyme called [[Polyphenol Oxidase]] (PPO) to [[Oxygen]]. This triggers a reaction that produces melanin, the pigment that causes the brown discolouration. This process can be slowed or stopped by:
- **Acidulation:** Applying an acid, such as [[Lemon Juice]] (which contains [[Ascorbic Acid]]) or [[Vinegar]] ([[Acetic Acid]]), lowers the pH of the fruit's surface, inhibiting the enzyme's activity.
- **Heat Application:** A quick [[Blanching]] causes [[Protein Denaturation]], permanently deactivating the PPO enzyme.
- **Reducing Oxygen:** Submerging cut fruit in water or using a vacuum sealer limits the oxygen available for the reaction.

## How to Do It
The exact method depends on the type of fruit. A sharp [[Chef's Knife]] and a stable [[Cutting Board]] are essential for all tasks.

**1. Pome Fruits (Apples, Pears):**
- Wash the fruit thoroughly.
- Use a [[Vegetable Peeler]] to remove the skin, if desired.
- Quarter the fruit with a [[Chef's Knife]], then use a [[Paring Knife]] or [[Apple Corer]] to remove the core and seeds.
- Slice or dice as required. To prevent browning, place cut pieces in a bowl of cold water with a splash of [[Lemon Juice]].

**2. Citrus Fruits (Oranges, Lemons):**
- For zest, use a [[Zester]] or [[Microplane]] to remove the coloured outer peel, avoiding the bitter white [[Pith]].
- To segment (or supreme), slice off the top and bottom, stand the fruit on the cutting board, and use a [[Paring Knife]] to cut away the peel and pith, following the fruit's contour. Then, carefully cut between the membranes to release the segments.
- For juice, use a [[Juicer]] or reamer.

**3. Stone Fruits (Peaches, Plums):**
- To easily remove the skin, score an "X" on the bottom and [[Blanching|blanch]] the fruit in boiling water for 30-60 seconds, then shock it in an ice bath. The skin will peel away easily.
- Cut the fruit in half along its seam down to the pit, then twist the halves to separate them. Use the tip of a knife to pry out the pit.

**4. Berries (Strawberries, Blueberries):**
- Wash gently in a [[Colander]] just before use to prevent them from becoming waterlogged and moldy.
- To hull [[Strawberries]], use a [[Paring Knife]] to cut a small cone-shaped piece from the stem end.

## Common Mistakes
- **Allowing fruit to brown:** Failing to use an acidic agent like [[Lemon Juice]] or a water bath for susceptible fruits like [[Apples]] and [[Bananas]].
- **Using a dull knife:** A dull blade crushes fruit cells instead of slicing cleanly, leading to bruising, faster spoilage, and increased [[Enzymatic Browning]].
- **Washing berries too soon:** Washing delicate [[Berries]] long before they are to be used introduces moisture that accelerates mold growth.
- **Leaving pith on citrus:** The white [[Pith]] underneath the zest of [[Citrus]] fruits is bitter and can impart an unpleasant flavour to dishes.
- **Improper storage:** Storing cut fruit in an open container in the refrigerator can lead to drying out and absorption of other odours.

## When to Use It
Fruit preparation is a fundamental step in countless sweet and savory dishes across global cuisines.
- **Raw Dishes:** Essential for [[Fruit Salad]], garnishes for cocktails, and components in fresh salsas or [[Ceviche]].
- **Baked Goods:** Required for fillings in [[Apple Pie]], [[Tarts]], [[Cobblers]], and for incorporating fruit into [[Muffins]] and cakes.
- **Preserves:** The first step in making [[Jam]], [[Jelly]], and [[Chutney]].
- **Sauces and Purées:** Used to create a [[Coulis]], compote, or fruit-based sauces to accompany savory dishes, a common practice in [[French Cuisine]].

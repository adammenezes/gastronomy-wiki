## Overview
Food Cost Management is a critical aspect of operating a successful [[foodservice industry]] [[business]], extending beyond the [[kitchen]] to impact overall [[financial health]]. [[Chefs]] and [[culinary managers]] are responsible for implementing [[systems]] and fostering a [[cost-conscious kitchen culture]] to ensure [[profitability]] and minimize [[waste]]. This involves understanding various [[cost control]] strategies and [[financial analysis]] methods to maintain sustainable [[operations]].

## Method
1.  **Build a [[Cost-Conscious Kitchen Culture]]:** Establish [[standardized systems]] for [[ordering]], [[receiving]], [[storing]], and [[production]]. Implement regular [[training]] and [[in-service sessions]] to ensure [[staff adherence]]. Utilize [[standardized recipes]] and [[procedures]] for [[consistency]] in [[production]] and [[cost]].
2.  **Calculate [[Plate Cost]]:** Break down each [[menu item's cost]] using [[standardized recipes]]. Sum the [[cost]] of all [[ingredients]]. Add the [[Q Factor]] (typically 5-10%) to account for extras like [[disposables]], [[waste]], [[accompaniments]], and [[condiments]].
3.  **Determine [[Menu Price]]:** Apply a [[markup]] based on a [[target food cost percentage]] (e.g., 35%). Adjust the [[actual selling price]] based on [[menu mix]], [[competitor pricing]], and [[customer perception]].
4.  **Calculate [[Overall Food Cost Percentage]]:**
    a.  Determine [[Cost of Goods Sold]] ([[COGS]]) for a [[period]] using the [[formula]]: [[Opening Inventory]] + [[Purchases]] – [[Closing Inventory]] = [[COGS]].
    b.  Divide [[COGS]] by [[total food sales]] for the [[month]] and multiply by 100 to get the [[food cost percentage]].
5.  **Implement [[Portion Control]]:** Develop and post [[standardized portions]] to prevent [[over-serving]] and [[waste]].
6.  **Utilize [[Production Forecasting]]:** Base [[prep sheets]] and [[par levels]] on [[sales history]] to reduce [[overproduction]] and [[waste]].
7.  **Manage [[Inventory]]:** Conduct regular [[inventory calculations]] to align [[costs]] with [[budget forecasts]]. Implement [[checks and balances]] to [[mitigate]] [[theft]] and [[waste]]. A [[physical inventory]] is usually done [[monthly]], but can be more frequent if [[costs]] are uncontrollable.
8.  **Conduct [[Purchasing Analysis]]:** [[Review]] and [[compare prices]] from multiple [[vendors]] to ensure the best [[value]] for every [[dollar spent]].
9.  **Train [[Service Staff]]:** Ensure [[staff]] [[charge correct prices]] and avoid [[unnecessary comps]] or [[re-fires]] due to [[poor service]] or [[spills]], [[protecting revenue]].
10. **Repurpose [[Leftovers and Trim]]:** Creatively use [[food scraps]] and [[overproduction]] for other [[preparations]] to [[reduce waste]] and [[increase profit margins]].

## Chef's Notes & Variations
-   The [[Q Factor]] is crucial for accurate [[plate cost]] [[calculations]], covering [[hidden costs]] like [[salt]], [[pepper]], [[sugar]], [[lemon wedges]], [[condiments]], [[bread]], [[butter]], [[garnishes]], [[disposables]], and [[waste]] from [[overproduction]] or [[improper cooking]]. It can range from 5-10% of [[food cost]], or up to $3 per [[plate]] in a [[sit-down restaurant]].
-   [[Menu prices]] are not solely determined by [[food cost percentage]]; [[perceived customer value]], [[competitor prices]], and [[menu mix]] also play significant [[roles]].
-   Industry [[food cost percentages]] typically range from 20-40%, with [[sit-down restaurants]] often targeting 30-35%. [[Caterers]] and [[banquet operations]] may achieve lower [[percentages]] (25-30%) due to guaranteed [[guest counts]] and [[set menus]].
-   Analyze [[raw dollar revenue]] in addition to [[food cost percentages]]. [[High-cost items]] like [[lobster]] might have a higher [[food cost percentage]] but generate more absolute [[profit]] per [[sale]] than [[low-cost items]] like [[soup]].
-   The [[foodservice business]] operates on very thin [[profit margins]], typically 3-6% before [[taxes]], once [[prime costs]] ([[food]], [[beverage]], [[labor]]) and other [[operating expenses]] are factored in.
-   [[Employee training]] is key to reducing [[waste]] from [[improper cooking]] and [[re-fires]].

## Overview
Thi Cao is the Executive Chef and Partner at Buckley’s Restaurant in [[Milwaukee]], [[Wisconsin]]. A Wisconsin native with a Southeast Asian heritage, Cao initially pursued a career in IT after earning a Business Management degree. At the age of twenty-nine, he made a significant career change to follow his passion for food, subsequently earning a Culinary Arts degree from [[Le Cordon Bleu]] in [[Los Angeles]].

His career is marked by experience in high-octane, [[Michelin Star|Michelin-starred]] kitchens under renowned chefs. He trained with [[Marc Fosh]] in [[Majorca]], [[Spain]]; [[Daniel Patterson]] at Coi; [[Laurent Gras]] at L2o; and [[Michael Cimarusti]] at Water Grill. His culinary style is described as "personality driven" and ingredient-focused, blending his Midwestern roots and Southeast Asian heritage with a fundamental training in [[French Cuisine]].

## Culinary Philosophy & Style
Chef Cao's food philosophy is ingredient-focused and "not fussy." His style is a unique synthesis of his personal history and professional training, combining the sensibilities of a Midwestern upbringing, the flavours of his [[Southeast Asian Cuisine|Southeast Asian]] heritage, and the rigorous techniques of classical [[French Cuisine]]. He operates with a contemporary perspective, prioritising a strong foundation in basic skills. He firmly believes that one cannot be a good cook without first being a great prep cook, emphasizing the importance of mastering foundational skills before advancing.

## Career & Accomplishments
After graduating from [[Le Cordon Bleu]], Thi Cao honed his skills in several world-class kitchens. His notable stages include working with [[Marc Fosh]] at Reads Hotel in [[Majorca]], [[Spain]], [[Daniel Patterson]] at Coi, [[Laurent Gras]] at L2o, and [[Michael Cimarusti]] at Water Grill in [[Los Angeles]].

Upon returning to [[Wisconsin]], Cao participated in the Chef Apprentice program at the Pfister Hotel’s Celia Restaurant. He also worked at Coquette Café under [[James Beard Foundation|James Beard]] award winner [[Sandy D’Amato]]. He earned his first position as Chef at Osteria del Mondo, where his work garnered four stars from the Milwaukee Journal Sentinel. Currently, he is the Executive Chef and Partner at Buckley’s Restaurant in [[Milwaukee]].

## Professional Lessons
Chef Cao shares several key lessons learned throughout his career, reflecting a philosophy of hard work, humility, and passion:

*   A good attitude is more valuable than a good resume.
*   Chasing passion and dreams will ultimately lead to financial success, whereas chasing money is a perpetual pursuit.
*   The customer is not always right, but it is the chef's job to make it right for them.
*   Recognizing a problem without actively working to solve it makes you part of the problem.
*   Mastering the role of a [[Prep Cook]] is a non-negotiable foundation for becoming a good cook; skipping basic development leads to a weak foundation.
*   Culinary arts is a field where one cannot fake their way through; skill and craft are paramount.
*   Watching instructional videos on platforms like YouTube is not a substitute for real-life, hands-on experience.

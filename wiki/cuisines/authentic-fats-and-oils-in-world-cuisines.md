## Overview
The choice of cooking fat or oil is a foundational element that defines the character of a [[Cuisine]]. Alongside indigenous ingredients and specific [[Technique|techniques]], the fat used—from [[Butter]] in [[French Cuisine]] to [[Olive Oil]] in [[Mediterranean Cuisine]]—imparts a distinct and authentic flavor profile that is central to a dish's identity. While modern cooking often utilizes neutral-tasting refined oils, traditional preparations rely on fats that are deeply rooted in a region's agriculture and history.

## A Global Survey of Cooking Fats
Different cultures have historically relied on either animal-derived or plant-derived fats, based on local availability and climate.

### Animal Fats
Animal fats have been a culinary staple for centuries, prized for their rich flavor and versatility.

*   **[[Butter]]**: A cornerstone of cooking in [[Northern Europe]] and [[North America]].
*   **[[Ghee]]**: A form of [[Clarified Butter]] that is essential to [[Indian Cuisine]] due to its high [[Smoke Point]] and long shelf life.
*   **[[Schmaltz]]**: [[Rendering|Rendered]] fat from chicken or geese, traditional in [[Ashkenazi Jewish Cuisine]], [[Polish Cuisine]], and [[Ukrainian Cuisine]].
*   **[[Duck Fat]] & Goose Fat**: Used extensively in [[Southwest France]] for preparing [[Confit]], a [[Preservation]] technique where cured poultry is slowly cooked and stored submerged in its own rendered fat.
*   **[[Lard]]** ([[Pork Fat]]): One of the most widespread cooking fats, used in [[Chinese Cuisine]], [[Thai Cuisine]], [[Nordic Cuisine]], [[Spanish Cuisine]] (as *[[Manteca]]*), and [[Italian Cuisine]] (as *[[Lardo]]*). It is also integral to [[Southern US Cuisine]], [[Mexican Cuisine]], and [[South American Cuisine]].
*   **[[Suet]]**: The hard, crumbly fat from around the kidneys of [[Beef]] or [[Mutton]]. It is a traditional ingredient in [[English Cuisine]] and [[Irish Cuisine]] for dishes like [[Steak and Kidney Pudding]] and [[Irish Stew]].

### Vegetable Oils
Oils pressed from fruits, seeds, and nuts are central to cuisines in warmer climates.

*   **[[Olive Oil]]**: The defining fat of [[Mediterranean Cuisine]], used throughout [[Spain]], [[Portugal]], the [[French Riviera]], [[Italy]], and [[Greece]]. Its use extends across [[Middle Eastern Cuisine]] (in [[Turkey]], [[Syria]], [[Jordan]], [[Lebanon]], [[Israel]]) and [[North African Cuisine]] (in [[Morocco]], [[Egypt]]).
*   **[[Peanut Oil]] & [[Sesame Oil]]**: Key oils in many forms of [[Chinese Cuisine]]. [[Peanut Oil]] is also common across [[Africa]], being indigenous to [[South America]] but widely cultivated.
*   **[[Coconut Oil]]**: A staple in [[Southeast Asian Cuisine]], particularly in [[Thailand]] and [[Vietnam]], as well as in [[South Asian Cuisine]] ([[India]], [[Pakistan]], [[Bangladesh]]) and [[Caribbean Cuisine]].
*   **[[Mustard Oil]]**: Known for its pungent flavor, it is widely used alongside ghee and coconut oil in [[South Asian Cuisine]].
*   **[[Rice Bran Oil]]**: Favored in [[Japanese Cuisine]] for its light, neutral flavor and high smoke point, making it suitable for techniques like [[Tempura]].
*   **[[Red Palm Oil]]** ([[Dendê Oil]]): A vibrant, reddish-orange oil with a distinct flavor that is essential to [[West African Cuisine]] and [[Brazilian Cuisine]], especially in the state of [[Bahia]]. It should not be confused with highly refined [[Palm Kernel Oil]].

## Modern Oils & Authenticity
Today, highly refined vegetable oils such as [[Soybean Oil]], [[Rapeseed Oil]], and its cultivar [[Canola Oil]] have become globally ubiquitous. Their neutral taste, low cost, and stable shelf-life make them versatile for many applications. However, while these oils are practical for everyday cooking, recreating global dishes with authenticity often requires using the traditional fats and oils that are native to their preparations. The flavor is in the fat, and using the correct one is a crucial step toward a genuine culinary experience.

## Signature Dishes
Many iconic dishes owe their distinctive character and flavor to the specific fats and oils used in their preparation.
*   [[Confit]]: A classic [[French Cuisine|French]] preparation, most famously [[Duck Confit]], where the meat is slow-cooked and preserved in its own [[Duck Fat]], imparting unparalleled richness and tenderness.
*   [[Steak and Kidney Pudding]] and [[Irish Stew]]: Staples of [[English Cuisine]] and [[Irish Cuisine]], these dishes traditionally rely on [[Suet]] for its unique texture and ability to create a rich, savory crust or thickening agent.
*   Dishes from [[Indian Cuisine]]: Many curries, dals, and fried breads like [[Puri]] or [[Samosa]] are deeply flavored by [[Ghee]], which provides a nutty aroma and high smoke point for frying.
*   [[Mediterranean Cuisine]] Staples: From [[Pesto]] to [[Hummus]] and countless vegetable dishes, [[Olive Oil]] is not just a cooking medium but a core flavor component, often used generously as a finishing oil.
*   [[Moqueca]] and [[Acarajé]]: Essential to [[Brazilian Cuisine]], particularly in [[Bahia]], these dishes are defined by the vibrant color and distinct flavor of [[Red Palm Oil]] ([[Dendê Oil]]), which is integral to their identity.

## Key Ingredients
While the page discusses a wide array of fats and oils, several stand out as foundational "key ingredients" that define the culinary landscape of entire regions and traditions.
*   [[Olive Oil]]: The cornerstone of [[Mediterranean Cuisine]], valued for its fruity notes, versatility in both cooking and finishing, and cultural significance.
*   [[Butter]]: A fundamental fat in [[Northern European Cuisine]] and [[French Cuisine]], providing richness and depth to sauces, pastries, and everyday cooking.
*   [[Ghee]]: A form of [[Clarified Butter]] central to [[Indian Cuisine]] and other [[South Asian Cuisine|South Asian]] traditions, prized for its high smoke point and distinctive nutty flavor.
*   [[Lard]]: Rendered [[Pork Fat]], a historically widespread and versatile fat in cuisines from [[China]] to [[Mexico]] and [[Italy]], contributing a savory richness to fried foods, baked goods, and savory dishes.
*   [[Duck Fat]]: Highly regarded in [[French Cuisine]], especially in [[Southwest France]], for its ability to create incredibly crispy textures and deep, savory flavors, particularly in [[Confit]].
*   [[Coconut Oil]]: A defining fat in [[Southeast Asian Cuisine]] and [[Caribbean Cuisine]], imparting a subtle sweetness and tropical aroma to curries, stir-fries, and desserts.
*   [[Red Palm Oil]] ([[Dendê Oil]]): Crucial to [[West African Cuisine]] and [[Brazilian Cuisine]], this oil provides a unique color, flavor, and nutritional profile that is indispensable to many traditional dishes.

## See Also
*   [[Smoke Point]]: The temperature at which a fat begins to break down and smoke, a critical factor in choosing the right oil for a specific cooking method like [[Frying]].
*   [[Rendering]]: The process of melting and clarifying animal fat, used to produce [[Lard]], [[Schmaltz]], and [[Tallow]].
*   [[Confit]]: A classic [[French Cuisine|French]] technique of slow-cooking and preserving food, most famously duck, in its own fat.
*   [[Maillard Reaction]]: The chemical reaction responsible for the browning and complex flavor of cooked food, which is significantly influenced by the type of cooking fat used.
*   [[Emulsification]]: The process of combining fat and water, fundamental to making sauces, dressings, and preparations like [[Mayonnaise]] or [[Vinaigrette]].
